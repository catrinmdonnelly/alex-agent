# Brand voice

*How your business writes. Alex uses this to shape meta descriptions, suggested page changes, draft outreach emails, and forum responses so they sound like you.*

## How we sound

Replace this with two or three sentences describing your tone. Are you warm and informal, or sharp and precise? Witty, or serious? Conversational, or technical? Be honest, not aspirational.

## Words and phrases we use

Replace this with words and phrases that feel like your brand. Specific is better than vague.

## Words and phrases we avoid

Replace this with the things that don't sound like you. Industry jargon, AI-flavoured phrases, marketing clichés, anything that makes you cringe when you read it on competitor sites.

## Reading level and length

Replace this with a note on how dense or sparse your writing is. Are paragraphs short and punchy, or long and discursive? Are you writing for experts or for someone who just heard about this category?

## Things never to do

Replace this with hard rules. "No emojis in meta descriptions." "Never use exclamation marks." "Don't write sentence fragments." Anything that's a clear deal-breaker.
